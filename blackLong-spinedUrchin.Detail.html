Black Long-spined Urchin

Double Click Window to Toggle 3D Viewing Mode

Common Name: Black Long-spined Urchin
Scientific Name: Diadema antillarum
Adult Size: 2in - 4in
Habitat: Tropical and subtropical marine environments in all three major oceans, primarily on coral reefs and rocky seabeds
Behavior: Nocturnal, hiding in crevices during the day and emerging at night to feed on algae
Lifespan: 4 - 10 years
Diet: algae and seaweed
Reproduction: broadcast spawning
Cool Facts: its spines contain an irritant toxin
Other Names: long-spined sea urchin, banded diadem, or lime urchin

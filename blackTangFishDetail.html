Black Tang Fish

Scientific Name: Zebrasoma flavescens

Common Name: Black Tang

Other Names: Black Longnose Tang, Longnose Surgeonfish, or Black Sailfin Tang

Behavior: Diurnal (active during the day)with adults living in loose groups, while juveniles are territorial

Habitat:Central and Western Pacific inshore coral reefs located around Hawaii

Diet: Omnivore; algae and zooplankton

Size: 8 inces, with males being larger than females

Lifespan: 10-30 years

Reproduction: External fertilization and scatter eggs

Cool Facts: The black tang is considered a rarity because it is only collected from a few specific locations, such as Christmas Island in the South Pacific
